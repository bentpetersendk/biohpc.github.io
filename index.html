What BioHPC provides

BioHPC combines project storage, user management, accounting, software guidance, and access to shared compute resources for Department of Biology employees working with biological data.

High-performance computing

Access to shared compute resources for analyses that are too large for laptops or local workstations.

Shared project storage

Storage organized around research projects so teams can work from common data and results.

Slurm job scheduling

Queued batch jobs help Department of Biology employees run repeatable workloads and make fair use of resources.

Bioinformatics software support

Software, modules, and workflow guidance for common life science computing needs.

Research computing support

Guidance for onboarding, job submission, data organization, and troubleshooting.

Project-based organization

Department of Biology employees work through Project Spaces that combine storage, membership, accounting, and access to shared compute resources under PI responsibility.

Who can use BioHPC

BioHPC is for Department of Biology employees at the University of Copenhagen. Access is organized through a clear PI-owned structure for research staff, technical contacts, and employed students connected to approved Project Spaces.

Principal Investigators

Approved Department of Biology PIs own Project Spaces and remain responsible for membership, usage, and compliance.

Research groups

Department of Biology groups can organize project storage, members, accounting, and compute access around shared scientific work.

Employees and employed students

Department of Biology postdocs, PhD students, research staff, technical staff, and employed students join approved Project Spaces.

Bioinformaticians

Department of Biology technical staff can support workflows and help teams use BioHPC resources effectively.

Technical Contacts

Delegated contacts can help review requests and support day-to-day administration.

Research support staff

Approved internal staff can support research workflows and Project Space administration.

How BioHPC works

Access for Department of Biology employees follows the established Airtable-based onboarding workflow: PI registration, Project Space request, user request, approval, and provisioning.

1

PI registration

The PI registers so BioHPC can confirm eligibility and identify the responsible Project Space owner.

2

Project Space request

The approved Department of Biology PI requests one or more Project Spaces for specific research activities.

3

User request

Department of Biology employees request membership in the relevant Project Space rather than joining BioHPC directly.

4

PI / Technical Contact approval

The responsible PI or an authorized Technical Contact reviews and approves membership requests.

5

Access provisioned

BioHPC provisions compute, storage, and login access for approved Department of Biology employees.

Project Spaces are the organizing unit

A Project Space is a shared project area that provides storage, user management, accounting, and access to shared compute resources for an approved research project under the responsibility of a Principal Investigator.
